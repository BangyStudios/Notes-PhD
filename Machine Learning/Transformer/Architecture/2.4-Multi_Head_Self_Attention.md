Multi-Head Self-Attention (MHSA) is a generalization of [2.1-Self_Attention](2.1-Self_Attention.md) that allows multiple self-attention heads to be computed in parallel. The idea of MHSA is that it is a kind of ensemble that helps the model jointly attend to information from multiple representation subspaces.

---
## Definition
Consider the standard [2.1-Self_Attention](2.1-Self_Attention.md) formula:
$$
\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}_\text{row}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V
$$
We can denote this as the following:
$$
Z_i(X)=\text{Attention}(XW_{Q_i}, XW_{K_i}, XW_{V_i})
$$
Where:
* $XW_{Q_i}, XW_{K_i}, XW_{V_i}$ are linear projections (i.e. $XW_{Q_i} = Q_i$) of $X$, respectively, where $X$ is the input sequence.
* $i$ is the index of the head $i \in \{0, ..., H-1\}$, where $H$ is the total number of attention heads.

Multi-Head Self-Attention (MHSA) is defined as:
$$
\text{MHSA}(X) = \left(\bigoplus_{i=0}^{H-1}Z_i(X)\right)W_O
$$
Where:
* $\bigoplus_{i=0}^{H-1}Z_i(X)$ is the "horizontal" concatenation of the attention matrix $Z_i(X)$
* $W_O$ is the output linear projection matrix of dimension $(H \cdot d_V) \times d_O$ where:
	* $d_V$ is the feature (not input) dimension of each head's value matrix
	* $d_O$ is the output projection dimension for the entire MHSA (a hyperparameter)
*Note: The projection is applied after concatenation so that information from different heads can be mixed.*

---
## Implementation Equivalence (Single vs Per-Head Projections)

In practice (e.g. PyTorch implementations), MHSA is **not implemented as separate projections per head**, but instead uses a **single full-dimensional projection**, since a single large matrix multiplication is equivalent to many smaller ones concatenated, but is significantly more efficient on modern hardware. Consider:
$$
Q = XW_Q,\quad K = XW_K,\quad V = XW_V
$$
where:
$$
W_Q, W_K, W_V \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{model}}}
$$

This is mathematically equivalent to per-head projections because the full matrix can be decomposed as:
$$
W_Q = \left[W_{Q_0} \;\big|\; W_{Q_1} \;\big|\; \cdots \;\big|\; W_{Q_{H-1}}\right]
$$
where each:
$$
W_{Q_i} \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{head}}}
\quad \text{and} \quad d_{\text{head}} = \frac{d_{\text{model}}}{H}
$$

Thus:
$$
XW_Q =
\left[XW_{Q_0} \;\big|\; XW_{Q_1} \;\big|\; \cdots \;\big|\; XW_{Q_{H-1}}\right]
$$

After projection, the tensor is reshaped:
$$
\mathbb{R}^{B \times T \times d_{\text{model}}}
\;\rightarrow\;
\mathbb{R}^{B \times H \times T \times d_{\text{head}}}
$$

This reshape operation **recovers the individual heads**, making the implementation equivalent to:
$$
Q_i = XW_{Q_i}, \quad K_i = XW_{K_i}, \quad V_i = XW_{V_i}
$$

## Example
### Head 0
Consider the example input weight matrices from [2.1-Self_Attention](2.1-Self_Attention.md), we compute the identity projection and subscript these with the head at index $i=0$:
$$
Q = Q_0 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
K = K_0 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
V = V_0 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}
$$
...
$$
Z_0 = \text{softmax}_\text{row}\!\left(\frac{Q_0K_0^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V_0 = 
\begin{bmatrix} 3.00 & 4.00 \\ 3.40 & 4.40 \\ 3.51 & 4.51 \end{bmatrix}
$$
### Head 1
Construct some projection at index $i = 1$, using the following matrices:
$$
W_{Q_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
W_{K_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
W_{V_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}
$$
Apply projections:
$$
Q_1 = QW_{Q_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
K_1 = KW_{K_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix}, \quad 
V_1 = VW_{V_1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}
$$
Compute $Q_1 K_1^T$ and scale:
$$
Q_1 K_1^T = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad
\frac{Q_1 K_1^T}{\sqrt{2}} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.00 & 0.71 & 0.71 \\ -0.71 & 0.00 & -0.71 \\ -0.71 & 0.71 & 0.00 \end{bmatrix}
$$
Apply softmax row-wise:
$$
A_1 \approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.20 & 0.40 & 0.40 \\ 0.25 & 0.50 & 0.25 \\ 0.14 & 0.58 & 0.28 \end{bmatrix}
$$
$$
Z_1 = A_1 V_1 = 
\begin{bmatrix} 3.40 & 4.40 \\ 3.00 & 4.00 \\ 3.29 & 4.29 \end{bmatrix}
$$
### Concatenation of Heads
Concatenate horizontally:
$$
\bigoplus_{i=0}^{1}Z_i = 
\left[Z_0 | Z_1\right] = 
\begin{bmatrix} 
3.00 & 4.00 & 3.40 & 4.40 \\ 
3.40 & 4.40 & 3.00 & 4.00 \\ 
3.51 & 4.51 & 3.29 & 4.29
\end{bmatrix}

$$
Here:
- Number of heads: $H=2$
- Per-head value dimension: $d_V = 2$
- Therefore, concatenated dimension: $H \cdot d_V = 4$

### Output Projection
Let the output projection dimension be $d_O = 2$, and define the output projection matrix:
$$
W_O = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad \in \mathbb{R}^{4 \times 2}
$$
Now compute the final MHSA output:
$$
\text{MHSA}(X) = \begin{bmatrix} 3.00 & 4.00 & 3.40 & 4.40 \\ 3.40 & 4.40 & 3.00 & 4.00 \\ 3.51 & 4.51 & 3.29 & 4.29 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6.40 & 8.40 \\ 6.40 & 8.40 \\ 6.80 & 8.80 \end{bmatrix}
$$
