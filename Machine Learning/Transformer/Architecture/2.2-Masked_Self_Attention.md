In a transformer, every token can _potentially_ attend to every other token.  
But in many tasks, this “fully open attention” is **not what we want**.

**Attention masks** are boolean (or 0/−∞) matrices that tell the attention mechanism:

> **Which token pairs are allowed to interact, and which must be ignored.**

The mask is added to the attention logits _before_ the $\text{softmax}$.  
A masked position is given a value like **−1e9**, which $\text{softmax}$ turns into **zero attention weight**.

---
## How the Mask Is Applied

Consider the standard [2.1-Self_Attention](2.1-Self_Attention.md) formula:
$$
\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}_\text{row}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V
$$

We can apply the mask by adding it to the logits before the $\text{softmax}$ operation:
$$
\text{MaskedAttention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}_\text{row}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}} + M\right)V
$$
Where $M$ has $0$ for allowed positions and $-\infty$ (in practice $-10^9$) for masked positions, so that $\text{softmax}(-\infty) \approx 0$.

---
### Example
Consider (from [2.1-Self_Attention](2.1-Self_Attention.md)):
$$
\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{2}} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.71 & 0.00 & 0.71 \\ 0.00 & 0.71 & 0.71 \\ 0.71 & 0.71 & 1.41 \end{bmatrix}
$$
Suppose we apply the following **causal mask** (upper triangle is $-\infty$, blocking future tokens):
$$ 
M = \begin{bmatrix}  
0 & -\infty & -\infty \\  
0 & 0 & -\infty \\  
0 & 0 & 0  
\end{bmatrix}  
$$
We add the mask to the scaled logits:
$$
\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{2}} + M
\approx
\begin{bmatrix}  
0.71 & -\infty & -\infty \\  
0.00 & 0.71 & -\infty \\  
0.71 & 0.71 & 1.41  
\end{bmatrix}  
$$
Apply softmax row-wise ($\text{softmax}(-\infty) = 0$):

- Row 1: $[0.71, -\infty, -\infty]$ → $[1.00, 0.00, 0.00]$ ("The" attends only to itself)
- Row 2: $[0.00, 0.71, -\infty]$ → $[0.33, 0.67, 0.00]$ ("cat" attends to "The" and itself)
- Row 3: $[0.71, 0.71, 1.41]$ → $[0.25, 0.25, 0.50]$ ("sat" attends to all, same as unmasked)

$$
A_{\text{masked}} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 1.00 & 0.00 & 0.00 \\ 0.33 & 0.67 & 0.00 \\ 0.25 & 0.25 & 0.50 \end{bmatrix}
$$

Multiply by $V$:
$$
Z_{\text{masked}} = A_{\text{masked}} \cdot V =
\begin{bmatrix} 1.00 & 0.00 & 0.00 \\ 0.33 & 0.67 & 0.00 \\ 0.25 & 0.25 & 0.50 \end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix} 1.00 & 2.00 \\ 2.34 & 3.34 \\ 3.50 & 4.50 \end{bmatrix}
$$
